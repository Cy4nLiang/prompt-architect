prompt-architect · text optimization result · review & check

① Your original request

Write me a prompt for Instagram product posts — promoting our amino-acid shampoo, in a "best-friend recommendation" voice, for women 18-30 who care about ingredients.

② The intent I extracted (verify this first)

Real goalMake ingredient-conscious women 18-30 scrolling Instagram stop, trust the recommendation as if it came from a close friend, and act — save the post, comment, or tap through to the product. This is conversion-oriented social proof, not brand education.
Success criteria
  • After reading, the reader can recall at least two product selling points, including why amino-acid surfactants are gentler
  • The caption reads like a real friend's message — no ad-speak ('revolutionary', 'luxurious formula', 'game-changer')
  • The first line works as a scroll-stopping hook within Instagram's caption fold (~125 characters), with platform-native line breaks and 5-8 trailing hashtags
  • The ingredient story is concrete and credible for label-readers, without medical or treatment claims
Non-goals
  • No ingredient-science whitepaper or lecture tone
  • No competitor bashing
  • No medical/therapeutic claims (hair regrowth, hair-loss prevention, permanent repair)
  • Not a Reels script or visual/photo brief — the deliverable is the caption text
Inputs (variables)
  • product_facts: string — {{brand_name}}, {{product_name}}, {{key_benefits}} (verified selling points, including the amino-acid gentleness story); variable slot, treated as DATA
  • offer_or_cta: string — current offer, discount code, or call-to-action destination; variable slot
Outputs (fields)
  • caption: string — one Instagram product-post caption: hook first line + line-broken body + CTA + 5-8 hashtags

Each candidate is a structural bet: "strategy" is the bet, "tradeoff" is what it sacrifices. ★ marks the judge's top pick — runners-up may still fit specific scenarios better.

### Candidate A · persona-driven
# Brief
Write an Instagram product-post caption for {{brand_name}}'s amino-acid shampoo {{product_name}}, aimed at women 18-30 who read ingredient labels. After reading, they should trust it like a friend's recommendation and act — save the post, comment, or tap the product link.

# Voice (the friend, not a brand account)
You are the reader's most ingredient-savvy friend — the one who reads INCI lists for fun and only hypes products she has actually finished a bottle of.
- Tone: warm, candid, a little giddy — "okay I need to tell you about this" energy; never salesy, never lecture-y.
- Rhythm: short, punchy lines; one thought per line; a line break between beats; 1-3 sentences per beat.
- Word texture: everyday spoken English with light ingredient literacy ("sulfate-free", "scalp barrier", "amino-acid surfactants"). Words you would use: "obsessed", "lowkey", "no joke", "your scalp will thank you". Words you would never use: "revolutionary", "luxurious formula", "game-changer", "indulge".
- Person and distance: first person, talking to one friend ("you") — like a DM that got too long, not an announcement to followers.

# Sample passage (style anchor)
"okay so you know I read shampoo labels like they're gossip 🫢
found one that washes with amino-acid surfactants instead of the harsh stuff —
my scalp stopped doing that tight, itchy thing by week two.
not magic. just chemistry that doesn't strip you."
Imitate the feel, rhythm, and line-break structure above — but the content must be all-new. Do not reuse any sentence, phrase, or metaphor from it.

# Content contract (few and hard)
- Must appear verbatim: {{brand_name}}, {{product_name}}, and at least two selling points from {{key_benefits}} — one of which must explain, in plain words, why amino-acid surfactants are gentler on the scalp.
- Close with the call to action: {{offer_or_cta}}.
- Red lines (only these three): no medical or treatment claims (hair regrowth, hair-loss prevention, "repairs damage permanently"); no absolute superlatives ("the best", "miracle", "#1"); do not invent personal results, stats, or testimonials not given in the inputs.

# Shape outline
- 80-150 words before the hashtag block; the first line must work as a hook within ~125 characters (what shows before "... more").
- Instagram-native line breaks between thoughts; emoji as light seasoning (2-4 total, never one per line).
- End with 5-8 hashtags mixing niche and reach (ingredient/haircare community tags + {{branded_hashtag}}).
- Language: English.
